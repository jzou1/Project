Mae’s work stems from her interest in Quantum Mechanics, specifically from an idea that consciousness determines objective existence. She is exploring this concept through a style caused MONO-HA. This creates a discussion how natural objects communicate in different spaces. From this concept Mae is interested in how the audience look at this communication as well as how objects communicate with each other. Mae’s research takes place at the seaside shores where she examines the bubbles that form from the sea hitting the rocks. To materialize this exchange of energy, Mae will create large plastic spheres representation of the bubble. The spheres will contain water and a carbon ball separately. The work will be interactive as the audience can push and move the spheres, to create drawing from the ball. This work will investigate how natural elements and people interact with the object and focuses on how the audience considering this relationship. Human consciousness affects the authenticity of objective facts.
